Oslo Kaffebar

is a specialty coffee roastery

Ever since we opened in July 2012 we have been busy making sure that we turn Oslo Kaffebar into a simple, comfortable space with an emphasis on some of the best home roasted espresso and filter coffees, home baked cookies/cakes and vintage vinyl you can enjoy in Berlin. Located at Eichendorffstrasse 13, near Nordbahnhof and the convenient S1, on a picturesque side street opposite a gloriously green park, Oslo Kaffebar provides a haven for anyone looking for an extremely well prepared shot of coffee before work, after lunch or any time at all. Oslo Kaffebar mixes Berlin’s concrete, raw interiors and exteriors with a Norwegian wooden weekend house. The mix is cool, natural and relaxed and we’re sure that you’ll love spending time relaxing at Oslo Kaffebar. Read more about us and our vision here.

Blog

Struggling with choosing which coffee to buy?

When I think about coffee, the first thing that comes to my mind is its aroma: The delicious smell that comes out when I’m grinding coffee or brewing it. It connects me directly to my senses, bringing comfort and nice feelings. That’s nothing better than starting the day with a fresh cup of coffee. The Specialty Coffee experience is all about flavour and what it takes to have the most stunning coffees. But what makes the coffee flavour? Flavour is a combination of aroma and taste. We feel it in our tongue, in our olfactory bulb, and nasal cavity. On the tongue, we perceive the basic tastes - acid, sweet, bitter, salty and umami (savoury). If we pay attention to what we are experiencing when we drink a sip of coffee, we start to notice general flavours like fruity, floral, chocolaty, nutty. With experience, we refine our senses and are able to taste and experience more. Just as wine, coffee tasting brings us a complex flavour experience. Before we drink a delicious cup of coffee, there are many factors that have an influence on its final flavour.
